Our premium coffee is lovingely grown in the hills of the Los Andes Reserve


The topography at Los Andes is very pronounced and the elevations go from 900 to 1,500 m.a.s.l. The temperature varies between 18º C and 24º C. The rainy season is from May to November and the annual rainfall is above 5,000mm. The soil, which is of volcanic origin, is very fertile and is well drained to absorb the rains.

Los Andes is one of those unique places in the world where coffee and tea grow on the same estate. Coffee has been grown in Los Andes for more than a hundred years. The Arabica coffee varieties planted include Caturra, Catuai, Java, Bourbon, Geisha and Villasarchi.

Our coffee was recently tested by the Guatemalan National Coffee association (Anacafé) and our cup profile was rated excellent (84.13). Our coffee has hints of: apple, grapefruit and citrus

The Los Andes Nature Reserve


In 2001, Los Andes was certified as a Private Nature Reserve and is a founding member of the Guatemalan Association of Private Nature Reserves (Asociación de Reservas Naturales Privadas de Guatemala – ARNPG). An initiative that started in 1998, the fundamental purpose of this association is the conservation of Guatemala’s natural resources. As a key step, members develop their environmental management plan combining conservation, economics (productivity) and human development.

Los Andes is a property where conservation, people and economics are working together for creating quality of life. For more than three generations several families have lived and worked in this property. This has created a team-work culture where families learn to be entrepreneurs and creators of a better standard of life, as well as pro- active participants in conservation and production programs together with the management team. Health and educational programs are supervised by FUNCAFE and AGROSALUD organizations.
